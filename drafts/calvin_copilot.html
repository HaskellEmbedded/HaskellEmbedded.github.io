Copilot Verification!

Posted on May 9, 2015 by Calvin Beck
Tags: haskell, copilot

Allo, allo!

In this series of posts we are going to be exploring the usage of Copilot for building and verifying Arduino programs. Some of my undergraduate research has involved writing experimental DSLs for generating formal models (both NuSMV and PRISM models), and executable Arduino code from one language. Let’s see if we can do something similar with Copilot!

One of the problems that I worked with for testing my DSLs was the orientation of unicyclic networks. The idea is that you are given a collection of identical processors running identical programs. These processors are all connected to each other in a network. This network can have at most one cycle in it. The processors then run a ring orientation protocol which they use to figure out a consistent notion of which processor is to the left and right of it. E.g., if you have three processors

A <-> B <-> C

Then A might be left of B, which means it also has to be left of C.

We’re going to use the algorithm outlined in Uniform Self-Stabilizing Orientation of Unicyclic networks under Read/Write Atomicity, which is actually broken into a couple parts.
